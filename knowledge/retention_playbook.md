# Retention Playbook

Interventions available to the retention team, indexed by risk driver.
Each section is a retrieval unit for the RAG layer.

## Premium affordability pressure

When the annual premium consumes a high share of household income, the
policy is at elevated risk of voluntary lapse. Preferred interventions,
in order:

1. **Switch premium mode** from annual/semi-annual to monthly bank draft
   to smooth cash flow (draft persistency is materially higher).
2. **Right-size coverage**: offer a face-amount reduction instead of a
   full surrender, preserving some protection and the client relationship.
3. **Review riders**: dropping ancillary riders can cut the premium
   without touching the base coverage.
4. For permanent products with cash value, explain the **automatic
   premium loan (APL)** and reduced paid-up options before the policy
   enters the grace period.

Never lead with "your policy will terminate" — lead with options.

## Early-tenure disengagement

Lapse hazard peaks in the first two policy years, before the client has
internalized the product's value. Effective plays:

1. A structured **onboarding call in month 2** reviewing what the policy
   does and confirming beneficiary details.
2. **Annual policy review** invitations framed around life changes, not
   sales.
3. Enroll the client in **autopay** during the first service contact —
   payment friction is the top mechanical cause of early lapse.

## Term product and conversion options

Term policyholders have no cash value anchoring them and shop on price.
Plays:

1. Highlight the **term conversion privilege**: converting to a permanent
   product without new underwriting is most valuable to clients whose
   health has changed.
2. At renewal cliffs, proactively re-quote and offer a **re-entry** or
   laddered coverage design rather than losing the client to a
   competitor's quote.
3. For variable products, schedule a **suitability check-in** after
   market drawdowns, when perceived value drops.

## Young policyholder engagement

Younger clients lapse more: incomes are volatile and the product can feel
abstract. Plays:

1. Digital-first servicing: paperless, app-based payment management.
2. Position coverage around concrete milestones (mortgage, first child)
   during reviews.
3. Smaller starter face amounts with **guaranteed insurability riders**
   beat oversized policies that get dropped in year one.

## Smoker and health-status considerations

Current smokers pay loaded premiums, which compounds affordability
pressure and lapse risk. Plays:

1. Offer **re-rating after 12+ months smoke-free** — a concrete premium
   reduction path that also improves retention.
2. Wellness-program credits where available.
3. If health has improved since issue for any client, initiate a
   **reconsideration request** — a lower rate class is the strongest
   retention lever available.

## Life-event and household changes

Marriage, divorce, new dependents, and job changes all reprice the value
of coverage in the client's mind. Plays:

1. Trigger a **coverage review** on any detected household change;
   under-insured growing households should be offered increases, while
   over-insured clients should be right-sized before they lapse.
2. Recently divorced or widowed clients need a **beneficiary review**
   call — it is also the highest-empathy retention touchpoint available.
3. Clients with several dependents rarely want less coverage; if they
   show payment stress, lead with premium-mode and rider options, not
   face reduction.

## Escalation and human-in-the-loop rules

Model scores route work; humans make the call.

- **High tier**: assigned agent must attempt contact within 5 business
  days; script suggestions come from this playbook but the agent chooses.
- **Watch tier**: automated educational touchpoint plus flag for the next
  scheduled review.
- Any client who mentions financial hardship is escalated to a licensed
  specialist — no automated offers.
- All outreach and outcomes are logged for model monitoring and
  intervention-effectiveness measurement (uplift, not just risk).
